G'day everyone

As mentioned last month, we have organised a small bus to travel from Launceston to Hobart for the linux.conf.au public Open Day. Open Day is a public exhibition where we hope to give a taste of the conference's highlights as well show off some fun and interesting things that people are doing with Free/Open Source Software. More information will be available on the linux.conf.au website</a> soon.


The trip will be free of charge, and will depart Launceston from the Birchalls carpark</strong> behind the Myer building. The bus will leave at 8:00am on Saturday the 24th</strong>, and return the same afternoon at approximately 5:00pm</strong>.


There are a total of 11 seats available, and once they're full, we will not be able to take further passengers. If you are interested, please contact me, or post a reply in the following forum thread</a> so that we can get a feel for numbers.


We are also hoping to man a Tasmanian Linux Users Group table at Open Day, as well as hold a round table discussion on what it's like to be a F/OSS user and part of the F/OSS community in rural and disparate environments.


This is a great opportunity for those of us who are F/OSS users in Tasmania to come together, share experiences and build our community.

I look forward to seeing everybody who can make it there!
