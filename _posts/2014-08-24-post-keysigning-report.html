Hi LUGgers,


So the keysigning event last night went well. The massive super beef

schnitzel defeated yet another of us. Fingerprints were exchanged, IDs were

verified, with about 10 people turning up.


Since a couple of people didn't have fingerprints to hand out last night,

we will be making every Hobart TasLUG meeting an open keysigning event, so

new keys to add to our web of trust will always be welcome.


Towards the end we tried out TAILS on my USB stick. The curse of the live demo

threatened when it didn't detect my wireless but on second reboot it all

started to work. Then we tried out Kali Linux which is the other distro I

keep on my USB stick, and while there was some mocking at the automatic

Gnome as root on startup, the included network analysis and development tools

included on the distro are impressive.


So, prepare yourselve for Software Freedom Day next month - Saturday 20th

September at Lenah Valley Hall!


Cheers,

Scott Bragg
