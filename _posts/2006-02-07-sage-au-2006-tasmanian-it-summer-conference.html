Registrations are now open for the 2006 Tasmanian IT Summer Conference

When : Monday 20th February 2006
Where : The Old Woolstore, 1 Macquarie Street, Hobart
What : The Tasmanian Summer IT Conference is a one day technical
conference and is the premier IT event in Tasmania. The conference
offers an educational forum for system administrators of all platforms
and levels of experience, and an excellent opportunity to meet, network
and learn. The conference provides a forum for Systems Administrators,
Systems Managers, Network Administrators, Developers of Systems
Administration Software and Managers of such groups to meet and share
their knowledge and experiences.

Speakers and topics include:
Building a Computer room 101, a light- hearted look - Geoffrey Day, TMD

Linux and the Enterprise- Richard Keech, Red Hat
Tools and Techniques for Managing Large Networks of Computers - Dougal
Scott, AAPT
Sudoku Generator - John Dalton
Enterprise Backup and Recovery, from Chaos to Cool, a Case Study at
Department of Education - Richard Eccles
Adventures in the Tar Pit, Implementing OpenBSD's spamd - David
Purdue
Deploying Defence-in-Depth: A Practical Guide- Daryl Sheppard
Wireless ISP in Tasmania - Nick Grundy, Tasmanet
Microsoft Active Directory Care and Feeding Sim Alam/Craig Smith
