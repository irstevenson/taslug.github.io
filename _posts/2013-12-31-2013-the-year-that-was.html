As 2013 staggers to a close and we look forward to a brand new year, it's worth taking a moment to reflect upon the contributors to our community throughout the year. There's a list of this years talks and speakers below but first I'd like to provide some special mentions:

PyconAU</strong>
For the second year</a> in a row, Chris Neugebauer and Josh Hesketh ran a highly successful PyconAu conference.
Linux Australia</strong>
Josh Hesketh was elected as president of Linux Australia</a>
Python Software Foundation</strong>
Chris was elected to the Python Software Foundation</a>
Statewide Meetup</strong>
Josh Bush organised the annual Statewide meetup</a> which was held in Ross, with a number of talks and TasLUG folk showing off their work (recap</a>)
Software Freedom Day</strong>
Both Hobart and Launceston (recap</a>) held Software Freedom Day events

For the Hobart meetings, Scott Bragg introduced a quiz based upon the Linux Professional Institute exams and a news highlights segment that added depth to out meetings.

Thanks to everyone who participated in any shape or form this year - you all made it the great year it was.

January - Bob Mesibov</strong> gave an introduction to what he called, "CoPa Scripting</a>"; the simple, yet powerful art of manipulating paste buffer contents before pasting to perform additional tasks such as creating a new file based on copied text, or removing hyphens and carriage returns from line-wrapped words (details</a>)
February - Mike Cruse</strong>showed off some of the locally designed and developed hardware and embedded software energy management/monitoring platforms developed by Definium Technologies</a>, a recent recipient of support from the Federal Government's Clean Technologies Innovation Fund. (details</a>)

May - Attitude</strong> gave a talk looking at comparisons of Linux Desktop Environments in a talk titled Objectively Choosing A Desktop Manager. (details</a>)
June - Cheeseness</a></strong> gave a talk on what it takes to get an Oculus Rift</a> head mounted display dev-kit behaving on Linux (details</a>)
