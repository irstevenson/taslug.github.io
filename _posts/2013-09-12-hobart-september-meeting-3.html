Welcome to September! I trust you've all recovered from the Federal Election DERP and have your eyes firmly on Software Freedom Day</a> which is less than two weeks away. Here's what's on this month:

When:</b> Thursday, September 19th, 18:00 for an 18:30 start
Where:</b> 69 Liverpool St, Hobart</a>. (Map</a>)

Agenda:</b>

  • 18:00 -</b> early mingle, chin wagging, etc</li>
  • 18:30 -</b> Question and
    answer session, new Hobart meeting venue, Internet privacy discussion </li>
  • 19:00 - </b>Hacking
    for Freedom: A TasLUG Sprint </b>
    As a prelude to Software Freedom Day, we'll be discussing and perhaps even making a start on contributing to a Free Software project of our choosing.

    ownCloud</a> has been touted as one project to work on, to help those who
    want an NSA free cloud.

    LibreOffice</a> has also been floated as another option, particularly as a demo for writing python / javascript macros.

    If you're looking to get a start in contributing to Free Software, this night will be a great opportunity.

    If you're an experienced hacker, you can provide mentoring and leadership to those following in your footsteps.

    If like me, you can't programme your way out of a paper if statement, it's great learning opportunity.
