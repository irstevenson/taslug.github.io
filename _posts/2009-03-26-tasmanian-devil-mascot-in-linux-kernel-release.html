One of the many outcomes from linux.conf.au was the incredible support shown for the Tasmanian Devil's deadly facial tumor disease during the Penguin Dinner Auction</a>. Amongst pledges, bids and donations, Linus Torvalds encouraged bidding by promising that Tux, Linux's penguin mascot, would be replaced by Tuz for the next Linux kernel release.

Last week, with the help of Rusty Russel, a patch containing Tuz was committed</a> to the source code, and yesterday, version 2.6.29 of the Linux Kernel [url=http://lkml.org/lkml/2009/3/23/449]was released[/url].

How exciting!
