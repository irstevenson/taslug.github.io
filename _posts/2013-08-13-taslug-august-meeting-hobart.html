Welcome to August! I trust you've all recovered from PyCon Au</a> and have your eyes firmly on Software Freedom Day</a> which is less than a
month away:

When:</b> Thursday, August 15th, 18:00 for an 18:30 start
Where:</b> 69 Liverpool St, Hobart</a>. (Map</a>)

Agenda:</b>

  • 18:00 -</b> early mingle, chin wagging, etc</li>
  • 18:30 -</b> Question and answer session, Software Freedom Day discussion</li>
  • 19:00 - </b>Coding the KickSat: Using GNURadio for a crowd funded satellite</b>
    The     KickSat</a> project was funded 12 months ago on Kickstarter and involves a 'fleet' of more than 300 tiny "sprite" satellites, each with a solar panel, sensors, microcontroller and radio. Project backers are invited to use cheap TV tuner USB dongles to be a 'ground station' to receive signals from all the satellites and relay their data to the Internet.

    As an aside, I'll be bringing along the     LEAP motion</a> thing for a (Windows) demo and possibly a quick look at hacking it to work on Linux, while we shake our fist at the decision by the creators to make it closed source.

  • 20:00 -</b> Meeting end. Many people go to the Brunswick for dinner.</li></ul>
